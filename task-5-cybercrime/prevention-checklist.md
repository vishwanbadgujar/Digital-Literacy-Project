# Part B: Prevention Measures

To protect against phishing attacks, users should follow proper cybersecurity practices and remain cautious while using digital platforms.
- Always check the sender’s email address carefully before opening any message
- Do not click on unknown or suspicious links, especially from unverified sources
- Avoid downloading attachments from emails or messages you do not trust
- Never share sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details with anyone
- Use strong and unique passwords for different accounts
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for an extra layer of security
- Verify website URLs before entering any personal information
- Prefer using official websites and applications only
- Keep your device, browser, and antivirus software updated
- Be cautious of messages that create urgency or fear to force quick action
- Log out from accounts after using public or shared devices
- Immediately report suspicious emails or cyber incidents

In India, cybercrime incidents can be reported through the official helpline number 1930 or the cybercrime portal.
